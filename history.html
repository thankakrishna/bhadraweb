The Divine Origin of Bhadreshwari Amman

Arulmigu Bhadreshwari Amman Kovil, nestled in the serene village of Kanjampuram in the sacred Kanyakumari District, stands as a timeless beacon of divine grace and spiritual enlightenment. This ancient temple is dedicated to Goddess Bhadreshwari Amman — a powerful manifestation of Adi Parasakthi who embodies protection, prosperity, and cosmic benevolence.

"The Goddess Bhadreshwari, whose divine gaze eliminates all sorrows, whose sacred presence transforms the land of Kanjampuram into a spiritual paradise — She is the Mother of the Universe, the eternal protector of dharma."

According to ancient Sthala Purana, the Goddess revealed herself at this sacred spot centuries ago to protect the devotees from evil forces. The temple's rich Dravidian architectural heritage has been a spiritual sanctuary for generations.

Samrakshana Seva Trust — A Legacy of Service

In the year 2004, devoted community leaders established the Samrakshana Seva Trust (Reg. No. 179/2004) to systematically preserve, protect, and promote the sacred traditions of the temple. The name "Samrakshana" meaning "Protection" reflects the trust's core mission.

Under the trust's stewardship, major renovation works were undertaken to restore the temple's ancient glory. The Rajagopuram was rebuilt with traditional Dravidian craftsmanship, the sanctum sanctorum was renovated following Agama Shastra principles, and modern amenities were added for devotees.

The trust has also expanded the temple's mission beyond worship — establishing educational support programs, free medical camps, Annadhanam services, and cultural academies that serve the entire Kanjampuram region.

The Kanyakumari District, where land meets three seas, has always been a sacred geography. Bhadreshwari Amman's presence in Kanjampuram adds to this district's spiritual tapestry, making it a must-visit pilgrimage destination.
